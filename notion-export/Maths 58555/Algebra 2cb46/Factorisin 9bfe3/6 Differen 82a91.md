# 6: Difference of Will Be Cubic

# **NOT IN LOG TABLES**

## Criteria for if the terms are eligible for this method:

- 2 terms
- Both will be cubic

### Examples of Cubic Numbers:

- a³
- b³
- 8
- 27
- 64
- 128

## General Form of a Cubic

$$
a^3+b^3=(a+b)(a^2-ab+b^2)
$$

$$
a^3-b^3=(a-b)(a^2+ab+b^2)
$$

## Example:

1.  
    
    $$
    X^3 - 27
    $$
    
2.  
    
    $$
    (x-3)(x^2+(x)(3)+(3)^2)
    $$
    
3.  
    
    $$
    (x-3)(x^2+3x+9)
    $$
